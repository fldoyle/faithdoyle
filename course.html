Who is this Ethical AI for?

Fairness

Fairness means ensuring AI systems treat all employees equally, with HR involvement in their design and deployment, and proper training before use.

Transparency

Transparency means ensuring people know when and how AI is used, especially in decisions affecting careers, and understand how it influences those decisions.

Data Privacy & Security

Privacy means AI systems must protect employee data, ensuring individuals feel secure in sharing information only when their privacy is clearly safeguarded and access is limited to trained personnel.

Responsibility

Responsibility means ensuring those who design and deploy AI are responsible for its outcomes, with clear ownership, organization-wide training, and ongoing updates as AI use evolves.

Reliability

Reliability means AI systems must consistently perform safely and as intended, with clear parameters, real-world testing, and human oversight to identify and correct any blind spots or unintended behaviors.

Humanization

Humanization in AI ethics emphasizes designing systems that respect human values, emotions, and dignity. It ensures AI supports—not replaces—human judgment, promoting empathy, fairness, and accountability in all interactions.
